My Journey to Zero Waste

Setting goals, facing challenges, and crafting solutions for a sustainable path.

My Zero-Waste Goals

Embarking on a zero-waste journey is a continuous adventure of learning, adapting, and growing. Here are some of the personal milestones I'm aiming for:

Goal 1: Embrace Reusables Everywhere

My Aim: Constantly try to use reusable bags, coffee cups, waterbottles and food containers when ever I am on the move at the same time slowly phasing out single use plastics from my life.

Goal 2: Drastically Reduce Single-Use Plastics

My Aim: Find out produce that are not packaged in single-use plastics and prioritize items with no packaging , or items packaged with metal or plastic packaging that allows me to reuse, or recycle those bottles or containers.

Goal 3: Master Home Composting

My Aim: Attempt to make a home composting system for my plants using old fruit peels and food waste so that I can start my own cyclic resource cycle in my house.

Goal 4: Cultivate Mindful Consumption

My Aim: Before purchasing anything, I must do the “ need vs want “ test with myself. I must also actively seek to repair my old items and apply the 5r’s , at the same time when I purchase items I must seek durability and hotswappability so that even if the item does get damaged I have the capability to fix it with parts on the market.

Navigating Challenges & Crafting Solutions

Potential Hurdles on the Path

  • The convenince of single use plastics on our society.
  • Limited availability of package-free or truly sustainable options in markets.
  • needing to spend time to research about Zero waste practices.
  • Changing personal norms and habits when it comes to using single use plastics.
  • diffrentiating between actual zero waste supporting products and greenwashing
  • Maintaining motivation and consistency, especially when facing setbacks.

My Strategies for Overcoming

  • Proactive Preparation: Plan meals and shopping trips to minimize impulsive, wasteful purchases.
  • Diligent Research & Community: Actively seek out local zero-waste shops and seeko out local communities that are big on Zero waste.
  • Confident Communication: Practice articulating my choices clearly and positively. Focus on the benefits and my personal commitment.
  • Focus on Long-Term Value: Emphasize the durability, reusability, and long-term cost savings of sustainable items. Prioritize essential swaps first.
  • Critical Thinking & Education: Learn to identify greenwashing tactics. Trust reputable certifications and independent research.
  • Embrace Imperfection & Celebrate Small Wins: Progress over perfection is key. Acknowledge small victories and learn from any missteps without discouragement. Focus on one or two changes at a time.
